      ## From Sessions to Studio

      MPL Box Fit grew out of coaching sessions at Monkey Playland.
      Mohammed Sadiq, the owner of Monkey Playland, trained with Umair and
      saw how well boxing fitness could work in the venue. They teamed up to
      create a dedicated studio in the premises: a focused training room
      where parents, juniors, beginners and regulars could learn real boxing
      without needing to walk into a big fight gym.

      The first posts from the build show the studio coming together in May
      2026, followed by 1-to-1 sessions and private group training through
      June. That is still the feel of the place: local, practical and built
      around people putting the work in.

      ## The Gym

      The room is black brick, rubber floors and hexagonal lighting, with
      mirrors running the length of the floor. The kit is the part that
      matters: a functional trainer with cable stacks and slam balls, racks
      of hex dumbbells, MIRAFIT weighted power bags from 5kg to 25kg,
      kettlebells, step boxes, resistance bands and a long line of TUF-WEAR
      bags down the wall.

      The studio is compact, practical and busy enough for coached work
      without feeling like a commercial gym floor. It suits 1-to-1 sessions,
      private groups, juniors and fitness classes.
